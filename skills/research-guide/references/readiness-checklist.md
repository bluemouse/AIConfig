# Readiness Checklist

Run this checklist before delivering the final research-report.

## Completeness
- The problem statement is clear enough that an implementer would not build the wrong thing.
- The primary user or stakeholder is identified, or the absence is explicitly called out.
- Goals and non-goals are both present.
- Must-have requirements are distinguishable from nice-to-have ideas.
- Acceptance signals exist for core requirements.

## Consistency
- The recommended direction matches the stated goals and constraints.
- Alternatives were not rejected for reasons that also apply to the recommendation.
- Non-goals do not conflict with functional requirements.
- Success metrics can plausibly measure the intended outcome.

## Evidence quality
- Source-backed claims include citations when sources were used.
- User-provided claims are labeled as such.
- Assumptions are visible and assigned confidence.
- Inferences are not presented as facts.

## Implementation-planning readiness
- Dependencies and integration points are named.
- Risks and edge cases include mitigations or owner/phase guidance.
- Open questions are labeled as blocking or non-blocking.
- The recommended implementation slice is narrow enough for a plan.
- The report does not contain code or detailed task breakdowns that belong in the implementation plan.

## Final gate quality
- If the report is partially agreed or blocked, the reason is explicit.
- If the scope was decomposed, the chosen slice is explicit.
- If a major assumption remains weak, its planning impact is explicit.
