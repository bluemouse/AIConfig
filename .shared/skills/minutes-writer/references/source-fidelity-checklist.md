# Source Fidelity Checklist

Before returning meeting minutes, verify each item below.

## Evidence and consistency

- Every participant, attendee, absentee, facilitator, minute taker, owner, date, and deadline appears in the transcript, supporting materials, or user instructions.
- Every decision is stated as a decision only when the transcript shows agreement, confirmation, approval, or explicit selection.
- Proposed options, disagreements, assumptions, deferred items, and unresolved topics are labeled as proposals, trade-offs, assumptions, or open questions rather than decisions.
- Every action item has an action verb and a concrete expected outcome. If the owner or due date is absent, use `not specified`.
- Supporting materials are not treated as meeting discussion unless the transcript references or discusses them.
- Technical names, acronyms, versions, issue IDs, component names, metrics, designs, and product terms are preserved accurately.
- No generic or plausible technical content has been added to make the minutes look complete.

## Meeting-minutes quality

- The minutes are organized by topic and outcome, not by every speaker turn.
- Discussion summaries are concise but preserve important context, trade-offs, dependencies, rationale, and unresolved issues.
- Decisions, action items, risks, blockers, dependencies, assumptions, and open questions are separated into distinct sections.
- Missing or ambiguous information is called out plainly instead of guessed.
- The final minutes are readable by both technical attendees and non-attending stakeholders who need the outcomes.
- The minutes use a professional, neutral, conventional style rather than transcript-like narration.

## Red flags to fix

- A table contains owners or due dates that are not stated in the sources.
- A sentence says `the team decided` when the transcript only shows discussion.
- A supporting document requirement appears as a meeting outcome without transcript support.
- A speaker's role, authority, or responsibility is inferred from context but not provided.
- The summary includes implementation details that are plausible but not present in the sources.
- A topic is described as completed, approved, blocked, or resolved without explicit support.
- Sensitive content (credentials, PII, HR, or legal details) is expanded beyond what sources
  support or redacted without user instruction.
